So we're in it again. After 24 months, we are back to the WTC Final and honestly there is no change. We are still scared and not many have hope of India winning. Why is that? Well, we have not been working for it. There are changes, injuries, IPL and what not. IPL has become the clear villain in these situations. Everyone loves IPL when its around but as soon as we are in International Cricket, we see IPL as the sole reason for Indian Team to lose everything. We loose in a consistent manner.

## Pre-Match

Much news about Australian Bowlers being scary have been comming. No surprise there. But seeing them after an IPL where bowlers had a tough just increases the anxiety.

Things have gone back to normalacy. [Dinesh Karthik][dinesh-karthik] is back to his commentating job. He's also become our remote correspondant to the Oval.

He is busy giving all the inside updates about the pitch. I don't know anything about the pitch. So I'll not even try to comment about the grass. I myself am learning about the impact that grass has on the bowling through the Twitter comments.(That's my goto teacher for Cricket)

**Memes have made their way to my feed.** Everyone is scared about the Australian Bowlers. Not much could be said from my side. I don't even know who is playing. Sometimes I feel that we have a serious problem of having too many choices. Now I should stop thinking about this and just wait for the match to begin.

## Day 1

The match has begun. The grass is there.(Still have no clue about it) Siraj has started off good. Hope he does well overall. Don't know why is it like this but everyone thinks that Australians will cross the line here. The opinion has been like that. No doubt about that. The reason? It's just their character. If WTC was there in Ponting era, there would be five more trophies in the Australian cabinet.

Umesh has bowled few overs and now everyone is discussing why Ashwin was not selected. Random twitter accounts are presenting Ashwin's stats which has nothing to do in this context. People are commenting about the team wasting an asset like Ashwin in such a big match. You know, the usual.

**Who is this [Travis Head][travis-head]?** He is going quick. It's always the unexpected people making surprise entries. No plan for him. Shami and Shardul claimed their first. Lord Shardul always brings in his absolute unexpected form anywhere he goes. They're going fast, like really fast.

Travis gets a 100! It can't get any worse for India at the moment. The impact of this knock is clearly visible in the body language of the players. They're getting more defensive with time. This is increasing the worries. Questions on keeping Ashwin out are increasing. Someone needs to give that breakthrough. Both the batters are well set. How to stay motivated at this moment?

Now this is too much. I can't handle this. I am signing off for tonight. The mood right now:

## Day 2

**Australia is at 327/3(85) after Day 1.** That's something. Hopes of winning the match are fading.

**It's 18:30Hrs and Australia is 463/8(118).** Indian bowlers did come back from the hopeless situation presented yesterday. But Australia are close to 500. I'm working today while watching the match. Some rare moments that made me jump off the seat but not much for India still in the match. India is leaking too many runs. It can't go on like this. An uphill battle awaits for the batters.

As I was writing the previous para, Siraj got Lyon bowled. I was about to write about India not able to clear the tail-enders and then this happened. I still think that Australia can reach 500 with one wicket left. Yeah, that is how we are at clearing the end.

**Australia bowled out for 469.**

India started off with a good intent but the top-order collapse at this point is inevitable. The usual collapse in important matches is executed well by everyone.

**Ajinkya Rahane** stands still in these difficult times. He's done this way too many times now. Everyone was surprised and critical about his comeback to the team. Jadeja did a quick 48 which was essential at that time. Now it's Rahane and Bharat on the crease at stumps.

## Day 3

Day starts and India has to bat for long. Half of the team is back in the pavillion. Bharat falls easily and now we are doomed.

But to get us all out of the agony, walks in **Lord [Shardul Thakur][shardul-thakur]**. Ajinkya and Shardul play a brave knock. Both get hit badly but still stay at the crease. Tough characters survive tough situations. Unfortunately, Rahane departs at 89. Just 11 short of a century. This knock really deserved a century. Now Shardul is the only batsman we can support. He completes a half-century and joins an exclusive club of players to make 3 consecutive 50+ scores at the Lord's. What an achievement for the Lord at Lord's!

Australian bowling attack quickly clears the tail. **India finishes at 296.**

Australia come to bat with clear intention to give a score they can not even think of chasing. Indian bowlers start well and the day ends with Australia's 4 down.

## Day 4

Day starts with a hope that India will finish off Australia under 200. Bowling starts good with a wicket. Then Jadeja takes out another one. But the real problem infront of Indian Bowling lineup - the tail.

Right now its 18:33 and Australia is 260/6 with a lead of 433. The match seems sealed now. Australia can start clearing space for the Test Mace.

**Australia declare at 270/8.** Target for India is 444 in remaining 137 overs left in the test

India is batting with a very good intent. Shubhman's wicket was a controversial one. But now India is one down. Pujara and Rohit are scoring runs at a good run-rate. This run-rate is giving us the hope.

Nathan Lyon got Rohit and Pujara also went back in the next over. This smells trouble. But now a very dependable duo is at the pitch. It's time for Kohli-Rahane partnership. If we want to win this game from here, then these guys have to score runs.

**Day 4 ends with India at 164/3.** India need 280 runs and Australia need 7 wickets.
